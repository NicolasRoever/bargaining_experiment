Price Negotiation

The buyer and seller negotiate the price. During the negotiation, both participants can submit a price. Once a participant clicks the "Submit" button, the chosen price is displayed to the other participant. Either party can revise their price as often as they want during the negotiation. The revised price is displayed on the participant's screen and remains valid until it is changed.

Upon receiving the submitted price from the participant in the opposite role, you can choose to accept their most recent offer at any time. If either participant accepts the other’s price, the match ends, and the product is traded at the accepted price. The price negotiation can continue for up to two minutes and will automatically end after that time.


Costs You Incur

The total costs you incur are given by:

Total Costs = Negotiation Cost + Delay Costs

1. Negotiation Cost

Negotiation costs (you can think of costs for lawyers needed for a negotiation) are incurred for each second of the negotiation. The longer the negotiation, the higher the cost. The total accumulated negotiation costs first rise rather quickly at first and more slowly in the end. You can see the shape of the function in the screenshot above in the left graph titled “Negotiation Cost”.

2. Delay Costs

For each second of the negotiation, the trade is delayed. This is also costly (think of a manufacturing plant which can only produce later because the raw materials are delivered later). The later you trade, the higher this cost. This is reflected by a reduction in the gains from trade by a percentage value. The current reduction is presented to you in the right graph titled “Cost of Time Delay” (the green bar shows the percent you can keep, the red bar shows the cost).


Summary

At any point in time, you have three options: (i) submit a new price, (ii) terminate the negotiation, and (iii) accept the opposite trader’s price (only available after the opposite trader submits their first price). In the end, we randomly select one match to determine your payment for the experiment.
